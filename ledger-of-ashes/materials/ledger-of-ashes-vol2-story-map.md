# ARC I — Flight & the Border
### *Chapters 1–6 · Midden gate → Valeris border*

---

## Chapter 1 — The Road Out of Midden
**POV:** Six | **Location:** Midden city gate → Southern highway

Six and Dreya leave Midden at dawn, the charred ledger wrapped in Dreya's coat and the warm iron box buried in Six's pack. Dreya explains what she's decoded of the ledger: a coup planned for the seventeenth, logistics from a Duke she won't name yet. Six maps the route south by sound — the rhythm of cart-wheels, the weight of iron in the city behind them. A stranger watches them leave from the gate archway. He does not follow. Not yet.

**Six's Toll:** Residual cardiac flutter from the Vol.1 finale. Tapping his hilt every few minutes to check the beat.

**Dreya's Toll:** Arm still healing. Memory gaps from the high-level Air burst. She repeats a sentence twice without noticing.

**Plot mechanics:** The ledger establishes the central stakes. The iron box radiates heat against Six's ribs. Dreya names their destination: Aunt Mira, last known in Valeris.

*Tags: ledger · iron box · departure · Vane foreshadow*

---

## Chapter 2 — What the Charred Pages Say
**POV:** Dreya | **Location:** Roadside inn, southern Accordance

Dreya decodes further pages by candlelight. Six eats. The ledger reveals encrypted shipping manifests for sun-stones being diverted away from the Church and toward Aethelian border garrisons. Dreya identifies the cipher — it's a Valeris noble house accounting code she learned at the Arcane College. She was six years old when Aunt Mira first showed it to her. The memory cracks something open she quickly closes again. She translates names to Six via hand-drawn diagram. He studies it, taps his hilt twice, and shrugs in a way she has learned means: *"That's worse than I expected."*

**Six's Toll:** None this chapter. Conserving.

**Dreya's Toll:** Moderate headache from sustained focus. Vision blurs at the edges. She drinks cold water and pretends it's fine.

**Plot mechanics:** Establishes the Duke of Cerdon as the ledger's author. The cipher links the Duke directly to Aethelian border raids. Dreya's expertise becomes indispensable.

*Tags: ledger decode · Duke of Cerdon · Dreya backstory · cipher*

---

## Chapter 3 — Vane at the Crossroads
**POV:** Six | **Location:** Border waystation, edge of Valeris

They reach the first waystation inside Valeris territory. A defector in a travel-worn Aethelian military coat is waiting — Vane, who introduces himself with the specific information that he has been following them since Midden. He knows about the ledger. He knows about the coup. He tells them the Magisterium, the Empire's mage-military body, orchestrated the Midden coup specifically to destabilize the Accordance — not to seize Midden, but to force the Accordance to look inward while the Empire moves on Valeris's southern border. Six does not put down his sword. Dreya asks seventeen questions in three minutes.

**Six's Toll:** Low-level current to his reflexes throughout the conversation. Fingers cold afterward.

**Dreya's Toll:** None — adrenaline is suppressing her migraine. It returns once Vane leaves.

**Plot mechanics:** Vane is introduced as an ambiguous ally. He provides the macro context the pair lacked. His motivations are unclear. Does he want the coup stopped, or does he want something from the ledger?

*Tags: Vane · Aethelian Empire · Magisterium · coup context*

---

## Chapter 4 — Iron Broadheads
**POV:** Dual | **Location:** Valeris open road, river valley

Ambush. Three Valerian soldiers — off-duty, paid freelance — hit them on the open road with iron broadhead arrows specifically designed to ground out magical defense. Dreya's air shield fails on contact with the first broadhead. Six's speed boost stutters in the open-valley iron content of the soil. They survive by terrain — Six uses the river embankment for cover, Dreya creates a vacuum bubble to suffocate a small area and buys them twenty seconds. They run. The box radiates such heat during the fight that Six's coat smolders slightly against his ribs.

**Six's Toll:** Mid-level speed boost stutters: blue lips, shaking hands. Seeks warmth at the next farmhouse fire.

**Dreya's Toll:** Air shield failure triggers full migraine. She navigates the next two miles on Dreya-stubbornness alone.

**Plot mechanics:** Establishes the anti-mage military doctrine of Valeris. Someone has already hired hunters — probably the Duke, who knows they left Midden with the ledger. The iron box anomaly deepens.

*Tags: ambush · anti-mage doctrine · iron broadheads · box anomaly*

---

## Chapter 5 — The Sun-Stone Trader
**POV:** Dreya | **Location:** Rural market town, Valeris

They stop in a market town and Dreya immediately spots a sun-stone stall selling dull, tampered crystals at full Church-certified price. She cannot help herself — she buys three, examines them publicly, and announces loudly to the stall-keeper that they are counterfeit, Duke's-taxed, and in violation of Church certification codes. The stall-keeper turns pale and calls the market warden. Dreya talks them through it using her academic distinction like a weapon. Six watches from a doorway, performing his best impression of a man who has never met this woman. They leave with a grateful trader's information: Aunt Mira was seen in a village three days east.

**Six's Toll:** None. Spends the chapter being entertainingly appalled.

**Dreya's Toll:** None — she is operating on indignation, which apparently cancels migraines.

**Plot mechanics:** Dreya's sun-stone authentication establishes the Duke's economic stranglehold. First real lead on Aunt Mira. Introduces the Grey Market survival economy.

*Tags: sun-stones · Duke monopoly · Aunt Mira lead · Dreya competence*

---

## Chapter 6 — The Warm Iron Box
**POV:** Six | **Location:** Roadside, evening camp

Six opens the iron box for the first time. He's alone — Dreya is sleeping, and his heart is still unsteady from the ambush. The box contains a sealed letter from Mara, written in her careful barmaid's hand on tavern paper. He can read slowly. The letter explains nothing directly — it says only that she found the box already warm when she found him, that she believes it belongs to whoever placed him in the Warrens, and that she couldn't bring herself to open the inner seal. There is an inner sealed compartment he hadn't noticed. He doesn't open it. He sits by the fire for a long time, tapping his hilt on nothing.

**Six's Toll:** Low — the tapping keeps the heart steady. The cold is emotional weather, not physical.

**Dreya's Toll:** N/A (asleep).

**Plot mechanics:** The box mystery deepens structurally: outer box, inner seal, Mara's note. Six's unknown origin becomes a live thread. The warmth cannot be explained by any known magical law.

*Tags: iron box · Mara's letter · Six origin · mystery deepens*

# ARC II — Minor Houses
### *Chapters 7–14 · Rural Valeris → Luminarch gates*

---

## Chapter 7 — House Eravel's Invitation
**POV:** Dreya | **Location:** Minor noble estate, rural Valeris

The village with Mira's trail leads them to the estate of House Eravel — a minor Valeris noble house engaged in a years-long legal dispute with the Duke of Cerdon over sun-stone distribution rights. The lord of the house, a thin, clever man named Eravel, has heard of "the Valeris woman who exposed counterfeit sun-stones in market." He offers them shelter in exchange for Dreya's academic testimony in his ongoing legal case. Dreya is in her element. Six is given a room with a fireplace. He spends the afternoon sitting in front of it.

**Six's Toll:** None. The fireplace cures the residual cold.

**Dreya's Toll:** Low — social performance is draining but manageable.

**Plot mechanics:** House Eravel becomes a temporary safe harbor. The Duke's political enemies are introduced as potential allies. Dreya's expertise creates leverage.

*Tags: House Eravel · noble politics · testimony · safe harbor*

---

## Chapter 8 — The Pike Formation
**POV:** Six | **Location:** Eravel estate training grounds

Six watches Eravel's guards drill at dawn. Valerian pike formations — sixteen-foot iron-tipped pikes designed to create a wall of cold iron that a charging mage cannot pass, combined with broadhead crossbows to strip magical shields at range. He maps the doctrine in his head the way he maps a fight: spacing, timing, weak points. He finds two. He demonstrates one to Eravel's guard captain, silently, by walking through their best formation with a wooden sparring sword and tapping three of them on the shoulder before they close ranks. The captain is furious. Six shrugs.

**Six's Toll:** Low — minimal magic, mostly physical. Keeps the coat on.

**Dreya's Toll:** N/A — she is in Eravel's library and has found three books she did not know existed.

**Plot mechanics:** Establishes the tactical friction Six will face in Luminarch. The pike formation weakness will matter in Act IV.

*Tags: pike formation · Valerian doctrine · Six tactical · guard captain*

---

## Chapter 9 — What Mira Left Behind
**POV:** Dreya | **Location:** Eravel estate library + nearby village

The trail leads to a village two miles from Eravel's estate. A herbalist there was Mira's contact. The herbalist won't say where Mira is — but she gives Dreya a letter Mira left for her. It was written eight months ago. It says: Mira has gone to Luminarch. She believes the Duke's involvement with the Empire is worse than political — she has evidence connecting him to God-Fall artefacts recovered from a border site. The letter ends mid-sentence. Dreya reads it three times. She doesn't cry. She talks instead — an unstoppable stream of historical thaumaturgy about God-Fall artefacts, their classification, and what it would mean if the Duke had one. Six listens. He keeps his hand on the box.

**Six's Toll:** None. He is being very still.

**Dreya's Toll:** Mid — the emotional weight combines with the cognitive load of processing the letter. She goes quiet after the talking ends. The silence is worse.

**Plot mechanics:** Mira is in Luminarch, alive (probably), with evidence that upgrades the Duke from political villain to something worse. The iron box and God-Fall artefacts are now connected.

*Tags: Aunt Mira · God-Fall artefacts · Luminarch destination · Dreya emotional*

---

## Chapter 10 — Eravel's Gambit
**POV:** Six | **Location:** Eravel estate, formal hall

Lord Eravel proposes a deal: he will provide them with documents identifying them as agents of House Eravel on legitimate business in Luminarch, cover identity, and one letter of introduction to a duke-level contact inside the capital — in exchange for a copy of the charred ledger and Dreya's sworn academic testimony delivered before the Radiant Throne's administrative court. Six's answer is a long, flat look at Dreya. Dreya's answer is a list of seventeen conditions. Eravel accepts twelve of them. An alliance is formed.

**Six's Toll:** None. The negotiation is Dreya's fight.

**Dreya's Toll:** Low — the negotiation energizes her. She is in her element when the weapon is words.

**Plot mechanics:** Cover identities acquired. Capital access secured. The testimony plot thread introduced — Dreya will have to perform publicly in Luminarch, which creates exposure risk.

*Tags: Eravel deal · cover identity · Luminarch access · testimony*

---

## Chapter 11 — The Duke's Lictors
**POV:** Dual | **Location:** Road to Luminarch

Iron Lictors. Two of them, on the road ahead — the Duke's imperial guards in full cold-iron plate, utterly immune to magic. They are checking papers at a checkpoint. Dreya's cover identity holds under questioning — she deploys Valeris-polite courtesy like a weapon and makes the Lictors feel they are being rude by asking. Six stands slightly behind her, very tall, very silent, performing the role of the "House Eravel enforcer" so convincingly that the Lictors don't look too hard. They pass. Dreya's hands are shaking when they clear the checkpoint. She tells Six she was completely calm. He doesn't correct her.

**Six's Toll:** Low-level reflexes current through the whole checkpoint. He is preparing to fight iron-armored men with a sword and no lightning. The math is bad. He knows it.

**Dreya's Toll:** Controlled terror. No Toll used — she didn't cast. The shaking is adrenaline.

**Plot mechanics:** Iron Lictors established as the primary physical antagonist in the capital. Six has catalogued their armor gaps. He found two. He doesn't like either of them.

*Tags: Iron Lictors · checkpoint · cover identity · tactical fear*

---

## Chapter 12 — What Courtesy Costs
**POV:** Dreya | **Location:** Roadside inn, one day from Luminarch

Dreya overuses courtesy as a resource and pays for it. Two local nobles at the inn recognize the Castellan name despite her cover — someone in Valeris still monitors for exiles. She deploys a full hour of Valeris-polite social combat, constructing a false backstory, flattering them, redirecting their suspicion, and emerging with her cover intact and her mind demolished. She finds Six afterward and says *"I need to be quiet for a while."* He nods. They sit by the fire. He taps his hilt. She watches the rhythm. Her thoughts settle. Neither of them acknowledges what just happened.

**Six's Toll:** None. He is doing the human equivalent of a cat sitting near someone.

**Dreya's Toll:** High — splitting migraine, nosebleed. Short-term memory gaps for the next three hours. She forgets she already told Six the cover story twice.

**Plot mechanics:** Dreya's exile and family history create active danger even under cover. The pair's dynamic shifts: she is letting him be still for her, without naming it.

*Tags: exile recognition · cover near-miss · Dreya toll · relationship dynamic*

---

## Chapter 13 — The Rhythm at Night
**POV:** Six | **Location:** Inn room, the night before Luminarch

Six is awake. He does not sleep well before something difficult. He sits on the floor by the window — a habit from the Warrens, where beds faced walls and windows were exits — and taps his hilt in the dark. He thinks about the inner sealed compartment in the box, which he still hasn't opened. He thinks about Mara's note. He thinks about the fact that his heart ran wrong for eight seconds during the ambush and he hasn't told Dreya. He doesn't open the box. He doesn't tell anyone. He finds the beat, and holds it until morning.

**Six's Toll:** None — no magic. The cardiac uncertainty is a pre-existing condition he is managing alone.

**Dreya's Toll:** N/A — she is asleep. She appears in the chapter only as the sound of even breathing through the wall.

**Plot mechanics:** Six's interior life deepens. His cardiac risk is established as a ticking clock — he doesn't know how much he has left. The box sits sealed. The beat holds. For now.

*Tags: Six interior · cardiac clock · box unsealed · pre-Luminarch tension*

---

## Chapter 14 — Luminarch at First Light
**POV:** Dreya | **Location:** Luminarch city gates

The white walls. The cathedrals. Tournament fields visible from the gate road. Sun-stone lanterns on every pillar, fracturing morning light into spectra. Dreya has not been home in four years. This is not home — she is from a minor house in a river valley two days east — but it is the same kingdom, the same light, the same language without an accent. She almost says something. She doesn't. She straightens her cover identity papers and walks through the gate. Six walks half a step behind her and to the left, which is the Eravel-enforcer position she explained to him three times. He remembered it.

**Six's Toll:** Low — iron buzz in the city walls immediately. He adjusts. The rhythm holds.

**Dreya's Toll:** Low — controlled emotion. The silver ring on her thumb.

**Plot mechanics:** Luminarch is established as a beautiful, dangerous place. Dreya's unspoken grief about her exile is present but sealed, like the box.

*Tags: Luminarch arrival · Dreya homecoming · city establishing · emotional restraint*

# ARC III — Luminarch
### *Chapters 15–20 · Inside the capital*

---

## Chapter 15 — The Grey Market Here
**POV:** Six | **Location:** Luminarch lower city

Their grey market survival skills apply differently in Luminarch. The monster bounty economy doesn't exist inside the capital — but construction of the new tournament field has uncovered something in the lower city that's eating the night-watch crew. Six takes the job alone while Dreya meets Eravel's contact. The "something" turns out to be a misfiled God-Fall containment vessel that has leaked. Not a monster. A magical environmental hazard he cannot fight with lightning. He improvises — uses his magnetic pull to collapse the vessel's structural crack and contain it, pouring mid-level magic into sustained precision for eight minutes. His lips are blue by the end. He collects the fee and finds a sunlit wall.

**Six's Toll:** Severe mid-level sustained use. Violent shivering, blue lips, numb fingers. Recovery takes two hours in direct sunlight — the southern sun helps.

**Dreya's Toll:** N/A — she is elsewhere.

**Plot mechanics:** God-Fall containment vessels are loose in Luminarch — connected to what Mira found. Six's improvisation with the magnetic pull will matter later. Establishes that the southern sun genuinely speeds his recovery.

*Tags: God-Fall vessel · grey market · Six solo · recovery*

---

## Chapter 16 — Eravel's Contact
**POV:** Dreya | **Location:** Luminarch upper city, private meeting

The contact is Lady Verasin of House Verasin — a Church administrator who controls the sun-stone certification records. She is exactly as sharp and exactly as dangerous as her position implies. Dreya deploys everything she has: the academic distinction, the Valeris-polite grace, the specific terminology of historical thaumaturgy, and one perfectly placed question that reveals she knows what Lady Verasin's certification records actually show. Verasin gives her fifteen minutes. At the end of fifteen minutes, she gives Dreya access to the records for forty-eight hours and says: *"You have the same eyes as your aunt."*

**Six's Toll:** N/A.

**Dreya's Toll:** Low-mid — sustained precision social performance. The reference to Mira lands like a blow she doesn't show.

**Plot mechanics:** Mira's trail confirms she was in the certification records building three months ago. Lady Verasin becomes a cautious ally. The Duke's falsified certifications are now documented.

*Tags: Lady Verasin · certification records · Mira trail · Dreya social combat*

---

## Chapter 17 — The Testimony Draft
**POV:** Dual | **Location:** Rented rooms, Luminarch

Dreya writes. Six reads over her shoulder, which he does not do with other documents — but this one contains the annotated ledger pages and Dreya needs someone to tell her when she's being too clever for the court to follow. Six's contribution to the draft: he points at three sections in sequence and makes a gesture that means *"I still don't understand this."* She rewrites them. The new versions are clearer. They work through the night. At some point she falls asleep over the papers. He puts his coat over her shoulders without waking her. It smells like wool and residual lightning.

**Six's Toll:** None — he's reading, not casting.

**Dreya's Toll:** Low-mid — sustained cognitive work. She falls asleep before the migraine can develop fully.

**Plot mechanics:** The testimony is the weapon that will publicly expose the Duke. The scene deepens their working partnership. The coat is not sentimental; he just has the only warm thing in the room.

*Tags: testimony preparation · partnership · night scene · character dynamic*

---

## Chapter 18 — Mapping the Lictors
**POV:** Six | **Location:** Luminarch streets, tournament quarter

Six spends three days watching Iron Lictors work security for the Duke's representatives at the tournament field. He does this invisibly — the tall mute enforcer for House Eravel has legitimate business in the quarter. He watches how they move in armor, how they compensate for restricted vision, where the gaps he found on the road are: the armpit seam, the back of the knee joint. He also clocks that they have a third gap he missed on the road: the visor pivot point. He files all three. He also notices that the Duke has eight Lictors in the capital — more than any noble house is permitted under the Radiant Throne's charter. He tells Dreya. She writes it down.

**Six's Toll:** None — observation only.

**Dreya's Toll:** N/A, but she immediately contextualizes: the Radiant Throne charter permits maximum four Lictors per noble house. Eight is a declaration of intent.

**Plot mechanics:** The Duke's military buildup is documented. The Lictor gap map will matter in the climax. The charter violation is added to the testimony.

*Tags: Lictors · armor gaps · charter violation · Duke buildup*

---

## Chapter 19 — The Certification Records
**POV:** Dreya | **Location:** Church administrative annex

Dreya has forty-eight hours in the records. She finds the falsified sun-stone certifications in six hours — the Duke's house has been systematically undercounting output to skim stones for export. She finds Mira's handwriting in the margin of one file: a notation made in the Castellan cipher, the family accounting code. It says: *"The stones are going east. The box knows where."* Dreya sits very still for a long time. Then she goes back to their rooms at a controlled walk and tells Six they need to open the inner seal.

**Six's Toll:** N/A.

**Dreya's Toll:** Extreme emotional impact, no physical Toll. The silver ring.

**Plot mechanics:** Mira was in these records. Her cipher note links the sun-stone smuggling directly to the iron box. The inner seal must be opened.

*Tags: Mira cipher · sun-stone smuggling · inner seal trigger · critical discovery*

---

## Chapter 20 — The Inner Seal
**POV:** Six | **Location:** Rented rooms, Luminarch

Six opens the inner compartment. It contains a folded map — a section of the Valeris-Aethelian border marked with three sites, one of them circled. It also contains a small shard of something that glows faintly and emanates the same warmth the box has radiated since Vol.1. A God-Fall fragment. Not a vessel — a fragment of an artefact itself. Dreya's explanation of why this is impossible takes eleven minutes. Six's response when she finishes is to pick up the map and tap his hilt three times, which she has learned means: *"I've made a decision."* He has decided they go to the circled site after Luminarch.

**Six's Toll:** Low — touching the fragment makes his residual lightning hum at a frequency he can't place.

**Dreya's Toll:** Low-mid — the theoretical implications are causing her to talk faster and faster. She forgets to have the migraine.

**Plot mechanics:** The God-Fall fragment is the spine of the personal mystery. Six's origin and the Mara mystery converge here. The circled border site is the Vol.3 hook.

*Tags: God-Fall fragment · inner seal · Six origin mystery · Vol.3 hook*

# ARC IV — Endgame
### *Chapters 21–25 · Climax, extraction, recovery*

---

## Chapter 21 — The Testimony Before the Throne
**POV:** Dreya | **Location:** Radiant Throne administrative court, Luminarch

Dreya presents. The room is full of Church officials, minor lords, and the Duke of Cerdon's advocates. The Duke is not present — which Dreya anticipated. She delivers the testimony with academic precision, the ledger pages projected via a Church scribe's transcription, Lady Verasin's certification records as corroborating evidence, and the charter violation as the final count. The Duke's advocates interrupt eleven times. She responds to each interruption politely, at length, and with the exact statute that makes them wrong. She is bleeding from the nose by the fifth interruption. She doesn't stop.

**Six's Toll:** He is in the gallery. He is very still.

**Dreya's Toll:** Extreme — she's holding back a full cascade collapse through sheer will. Nosebleed midway through. Heart palpitations in the final ten minutes. She finishes standing.

**Plot mechanics:** The testimony lands. The court opens an investigation. The Duke's advocates call it slanderous. Enough doubt is sown to freeze the Duke's political operations — temporarily. He will move fast now.

*Tags: testimony · court · Radiant Throne · Dreya triumph · toll cost*

---

## Chapter 22 — Extraction
**POV:** Six | **Location:** Court building → Luminarch streets

The Duke's Lictors are waiting when they exit the court. Not to arrest — the court action makes that impossible — but to escort them "safely" to a house of the Duke's choosing, which both of them understand is a death sentence in a nice room. Six declines the escort by walking in a different direction. The Lictors follow. What follows is a forty-minute chase through Luminarch's tournament quarter — Six using the architecture, his map of their armor gaps, and exactly one mid-level speed boost to create enough distance. Dreya is essentially unconscious on her feet from the Toll. He carries the ledger. He half-carries her.

**Six's Toll:** Mid-level speed boost: violent shivering, blue lips. He does not stop moving.

**Dreya's Toll:** Extreme aftermath — she's in collapse-adjacent state. Memory gaps. She calls him Mira once. He doesn't mention it.

**Plot mechanics:** The chase is the action climax. The Duke has declared active hostility. Vane reappears, opening a route they didn't know existed.

*Tags: extraction · Lictors chase · Six toll · Dreya collapse · Vane return*

---

## Chapter 23 — Vane's Route
**POV:** Dual | **Location:** Luminarch undercity

Vane's route is through an Aethelian defector network built in Luminarch's undercity — tunnels predating the cathedral construction, maintained by people who need to move without being seen. He explains while moving: the Magisterium wants the Duke stopped too, now that his operation has become visible enough to threaten the Empire's own plausible deniability. He is offering them extraction from Luminarch, not out of altruism, but because their testimony serves his former employers' interests in a way that doesn't require him to say so directly. Six watches him the entire time. Dreya asks seven questions. He answers three.

**Six's Toll:** Low — sustained reflex current while navigating the undercity.

**Dreya's Toll:** Recovering. Words are still slipping. She compensates by writing things down.

**Plot mechanics:** Vane's allegiance is clarified as pragmatic, not trustworthy. The undercity route gets them out. The Magisterium's interest in the Duke is a structural complication for Vol.3.

*Tags: Vane · undercity · defector network · Magisterium interest*

---

## Chapter 24 — The Duke's Final Move
**POV:** Six | **Location:** City gates, Luminarch

The Duke has blocked the gates — not with Lictors, but with a formal writ of detention issued under an obscure Valerian statute about "material witnesses in noble charter disputes." It's technically legal. It will hold long enough. Six evaluates the gate guards: not Lictors, regular Valerian soldiers with pikes. He counts the formation. He finds his rhythm. He looks at Dreya and makes the gesture she has learned means *"this is going to hurt me, don't watch if you don't want to."* She watches. He goes through the formation at speed — the first boost punches through the pike line, the second carries them past the gate, the third he doesn't have. He collapses twelve feet past the city wall. She catches him.

**Six's Toll:** Extreme — three rapid speed boosts in sequence. Cardiac Strain: heart lurches badly, arrhythmia, vision narrows. He cannot move for four minutes. His lips are gray.

**Dreya's Toll:** Moderate — she catches him with a controlled air cushion. Nosebleed. She sits on the ground and holds the beat for him by tapping on his chest until his heart settles.

**Plot mechanics:** The escape costs Six severely. The cardiac risk becomes undeniable. Dreya holding the beat for him is the emotional and thematic climax of the volume.

*Tags: gate escape · extreme toll · cardiac strain · Dreya catches Six · thematic climax*

---

## Chapter 25 — Return to the Sun-Stones
**POV:** Dreya | **Location:** Valeris countryside, heading east

Six recovers slowly over three days on the road east — the southern sun helps more than anything Dreya can offer medically. She watches his color come back by degrees. She does not tell him she counted the seconds his heart was running wrong. It was twenty-three. She does not tell him about the map's circled site, which she studied while he slept, and which is three weeks east of here, and which — based on the God-Fall literature in Eravel's library — may be the origin point of the fragment in the box. She tells him when he is ready. For now, she tells him the Duke's investigation is proceeding. She tells him Lady Verasin sent word that Mira's name appeared in a second record from six years ago. She tells him everything except the thing that would make him worry. He looks at her and makes a gesture she has never seen before. She doesn't know what it means. She asks. He says: *"Thank you."* He hasn't said a word in three days.

**Six's Toll:** Recovering. The beat is unsteady but present. He will not push magic for at least a week.

**Dreya's Toll:** Mild residual. She is mostly tired in the way that isn't physical.

**Plot mechanics:** The Duke's thread is resolved (temporarily). Mira is alive and traceable. The God-Fall fragment and the border site are the Vol.3 engine. The final image: Six's first words in three days.

*Tags: resolution · recovery · Vol.3 setup · Six speaks · Mira alive · closing image*
