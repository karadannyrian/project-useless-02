# Chapter Fourteen — The Road Out

---

Dawn at the eastern gate. The city was behind them—the Docks, the Warrens, the Eyrie just visible above the morning fog—and the basin stretched ahead in the early light: open country, farms, roads losing themselves in the distance.

Dreya was beside him, limping with the particular patience of someone who had decided to stop complaining about it. Her coat was drawn close against the gate's shadow. She looked at the road for a moment, at the empty morning it led into.

"Where do we go?"

He thought about it. East was Valeris. North was Frostmark. West was the Empire. South was the river and everything past it. Every direction had something waiting that wanted them dead or delivered. Every direction was a commitment to a problem he hadn't fully accounted for yet.

"Somewhere with fewer people who want us dead."

She laughed.

Small. Almost nothing—more exhale than sound, a brief compression of breath that found its way out without quite becoming noise. It lasted a second and then it was gone. It wasn't funny. She knew it wasn't funny. It was just what was left after everything else had been spent.

Then her hand found his arm.

He'd already seen it.

A figure in the archway's shadow—still, set back from the threshold, watching them with the patience of someone who had been watching long enough to be made and had decided to wait anyway. Standing in the cold of the overhang. Not moving toward them. Not moving at all.

He counted exits. The road ahead. The city behind. The figure standing in the only passage between.

His hand didn't go to his sword.

Not yet.

The figure stepped forward.
