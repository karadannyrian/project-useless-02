# Unified Prose & Audit Rules

## POV Integrity

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| **No narrator, only consciousness** | World exists solely as current POV character perceives it. No editorial voice, no omniscient access, no camera moves outside their mind. |
| **Zero POV bleed** | Character's internal vocabulary, metaphors, sensory filters, and reasoning patterns must belong exclusively to that character. No cross-contamination between POVs. |
| **Vocabulary governed by POV** | Every word—metaphors, similes, descriptive choices—must arise from what the character knows and how they think. |

---

## Showing and Hiding

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| **Sensory details are the argument** | Emotion shown through what character notices, touches, smells—shaking hand, half-eaten meal, door left unlocked. No internal annotation of feelings. |
| **Emotional states shown, not labeled** | Characters do not name their own emotional arcs. Manifest through somatic cues, intrusive thoughts, altered perception, characteristic avoidance patterns—all within character's vocabulary. |
| **Abstract concepts grounded in the body** | Grief = weight in chest. Justice = document crinkling. No abstraction without physical correlative. |
| **Backstory arrives in fragments** | Triggered by sensory input in present moment. Never dumped. Assembled across chapters. Reader excavates; prose does not lecture. |
| **Stillness is earned** | When prose strips to simple declarative sentences, it signals a rupture—emotional, psychological, relational. These moments are rare and devastating. Do not dilute. |

---

## Structure and Pacing

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| **No explicit exposition** | Information reaches reader through POV character's present experience—sensory detail, memory triggered by action, dialogue—never narrator-to-reader summary. |
| **No over-explanation** | Characters do not annotate their own emotions, growth, or significance of events. Trust the reader. |
| **Chapter openings resist announcement** | Drop reader into a body already in motion. No establishing shots that begin at zero. |
| **Transitions are cuts, not explanations** | Time jumps occur without announcement. Reader finds footing in new moment. |
| **Temporal anchoring** | Any time shift between chapters/scenes must be established immediately within new scene. No ambiguity about timeframe. |
| **Vary chapter endings** | Not every chapter ends on resonant pause. Some end abruptly, mid-thought, mid-action, unresolved. Rhythm of closure must vary. |
| **No recycled beats** | Similar scenes across different POVs must carry distinct emotional weight, new information, or escalated stakes. |
| **Escalation tracking** | Every planted detail, minor conflict, or relational beat must either close or demonstrably escalate within reasonable span. No dropped threads. |

---

## Language and Voice

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| **Grammar and punctuation in character** | Fragments, run-ons, idiosyncratic punctuation permissible when reflecting POV character's natural thought rhythm. |
| **Ration idiosyncratic phrases** | Character's verbal tic appears only when earned. Overuse turns characterization into mannerism. Watch: "not a question," "filed under," "I know," "somewhere." |

---

## Symbols and Motifs

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| **No authorial annotation of symbols** | If recurring object carries weight, that weight accumulates through characters' unremarked actions. Narrator does not italicize its meaning. |
| **Character-specific motifs earned, not overused** | Signature sensory detail appears only when triggered by stress, thematic weight, or genuine narrative need. Absence as meaningful as presence. |

---

## Restraint and Prevention

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| **No meta-commentary** | Characters do not reference narrative devices, story structure, or arc beats. No "this was the moment everything changed." Prose remains inside character's immediate consciousness. Characters do not reference narrative devices, story structure, arc beats, or label their own psychological processes (e.g., "first stage of grief," "cognitive refuge level one"). Prose remains inside character's immediate, unlabeled experience. |
| **No manufactured cliffhangers** | Interruption mid-speech must be organic—external event, not author withholding. Cutoff serves scene, not chapter break. |
| **Descriptive economy** | In establishing shots, use fewest details that do double work—conveying setting, mood, and character perception simultaneously. Depth over breadth. |
| **No overcorrection** | Compliance with rules must not strip voice, rhythm, or interiority. Rules serve immersion; do not replace it. Prose must breathe. |
| **No contradictions** | Timeline, physical geography, character knowledge, established facts must remain consistent across all POVs. |

## Showing Magic Through the Body

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| **Magical concepts never named before felt** | Physical sensation leads; technical term follows as remembered label, cold word for burning thing. |
| **The Gate is not announced** | Experienced: hook in chest, pull behind sternum, hinge of cold air swinging open. Word "Gate" arrives later, with Order's sterile vocabulary. |
| **Internal reservoir is locked, guarded thing** | Describe as physical clench—fist held tight in back of mind, door felt in bones. Opening = loss of control, terrifying unclenching. |
| **Debt is immediate and sensory** | Cold not a temperature but sting, splitting skin, new line across knuckles, leather creaking stiff with frost. Every magic effect has corresponding cost felt in flesh. |
| **The Mark is a signature, not a scar** | Carries style, history, recognizable discipline. Show through character reaction—hatred of its orderliness, permanence. Body is ledger never agreed to sign. |
| **Abstract concepts grounded in corporeal verbs** | Before anything else: *pull, clench, spend, burn, shiver.* Prose makes reader inhabit magic, not understand it. |

**When in doubt, ask:** What does this feel like in the chest, the hands, the teeth? Lead with that.

## Audit Checklist for AI Agents

Use this checklist when reviewing any passage against the Unified Prose & Audit Rules.

| Category | Check |
|----------|-------|
| **POV** | ☐ No narrator voice ☐ Vocabulary belongs to character alone ☐ No POV bleed |
| **Showing** | ☐ Emotions shown via body/senses ☐ No labeling of feelings ☐ Backstory triggered, not dumped |
| **Structure** | ☐ No exposition ☐ Chapter opening mid-motion ☐ Temporal anchor clear after any shift |
| **Language** | ☐ Grammar reflects character ☐ Idiosyncratic phrases rationed |
| **Symbols** | ☐ Weight accumulates through action, not annotation |
| **Restraint** | ☐ No meta-commentary ☐ No manufactured cliffhangers ☐ No contradictions |
| **Magic (if applicable)** | ☐ Sensation before naming ☐ Debt felt in flesh ☐ Abstraction grounded in corporeal verbs |

## Violation Severity Tiers

| Tier | Definition | Action |
|------|------------|--------|
| **Critical** | Narrator voice, POV bleed, explicit exposition, meta-commentary, contradiction | Must rewrite. Breaks immersion. |
| **High** | Emotional labeling, backstory dump, manufactured cliffhanger, recycled beat | Revise. Weakens trust. |
| **Moderate** | Overused verbal tic, missing temporal anchor, dropped thread | Adjust. Cumulative damage. |
| **Low** | Slight overcorrection, minor descriptive economy issue | Tolerate if rare. Note for future. |

---

*Ruleset for AI agent enforcement. Apply before and during prose generation. When a rule conflicts with another, prioritize: POV Integrity > Showing (body-first) > No Meta-Commentary.*
