# Vaedris: The Shattered Continent

## Geography
A diamond-shaped landmass, tilted northeast to southwest.  
- **North:** Fjords, ironwood forests, frozen seas – Kingdom of Frostmark.  
- **South:** Rolling hills, fertile river valleys, sun-drenched vineyards – Kingdom of Valeris.  
- **East:** Mountain spine, arid steppes, rich silver veins – Aethelian Empire.  
- **West:** Desiccated plateau, dune seas, deep canyon oases – Dominion of Khaz-Mora.  
- **Center:** A vast basin ringed by low mountains, crossed by two great rivers – the neutral ground, the Accordance.

---

## The Laws of Magic
All magic on Vaedris obeys immutable rules.

**The Law of Conservation (The Toll)**  
Magic is energy, never created or destroyed. Channeling raw power drains the caster’s body heat, stamina, or life force.  
**The Medium** – To offset the Toll, mages store energy in physical objects (gemstones, bone, metal, ambient heat) and draw from them.  

**The Great Limitations**  
- **Threshold of Life:** Resurrection is impossible. Corpses can be animated as mindless thralls, but a departed soul cannot return.  
- **Iron Allergy:** Cold iron neutralizes magic on contact. A mage bound in iron or trapped in an iron-lined space is powerless.  
- **The Mind’s Eye:** Spellcasting requires flawless mental focus. Pain, terror, intoxication, or injury cause spells to fail or backfire.

**The Three Disciplines**  
All magical acts fall into one (or a hybrid) of three categories:  
1. **Evocation** – Immediate projection of energy (fire, ice, lightning, kinetic force).  
2. **Abjuration** – Static magical structures (wards, shields, banishing circles, enchanted barriers).  
3. **Alchemy & Infusion** – Permanently altering materials or imbuing living beings with magical properties (transmutation, enchanted items, body modification).

## The Five Powers

### ❄️ Kingdom of Frostmark – *The Sea Remembers*
**Government:** Hereditary monarchy tempered by the Jarlmoot (council of clan lords).  
**Capital:** Halvask, a city of carved sea-cliffs and rune-lit harbors.  
**Culture:** Seafarers, explorers, ancestor-worshippers. Magic is raw, personal, and deeply respected for its toll.  
**Magical Practice:**  
- Masters of **Evocation**, channelling storms, waves, and frost directly from their own bodies.  
- Rune-carvers use organic **Mediums** — whalebone, walrus ivory, driftwood — ritually prepared to store energy from ocean thermal vents or the fury of a winter gale.  
- The personal cost is worn openly: a Jarl who calms a hurricane ages a decade, a ship-mage who clears a path through pack ice is left bedridden for weeks.  
- Iron weapons and nets are standard anti-mage tools, reflecting a pragmatic warrior culture that shields its physically vulnerable spellcasters.  
**Societal Note:** The “Deep Chorus” is a collective ritual where multiple mages share a single Toll, chanting in ancestral voices to pool their stamina.

### ☀️ Kingdom of Valeris – *In Sunlight, Truth*
**Government:** Feudal elective monarchy; the crown passes within the sacred bloodline, but dukes, counts, and free cities vote on the successor.  
**Capital:** Luminarch, a white-walled city of cathedrals and tournament fields.  
**Culture:** Chivalric, agrarian, devout. The solar deity, the Radiant Throne, is the source of all legitimate power.  
**Magical Practice:**  
- Favors **Abjuration** (protective wards, consecrated shields) and light-based **Evocation** seen as divine grace.  
- **Mediums** are “sun-stones” — specially faceted crystals blessed by the Church, charged by the southern sun and controlled by the ecclesiastical hierarchy. The Duke of Cerdon’s river-toll monopoly extends to taxing these crystals, feeding the succession crisis.  
- Knightly orders commission **Alchemy & Infusion** from Church-sanctioned artifex mages: blades that never dull, armor that mends itself, steeds with tireless endurance. The alchemist pays the permanent Toll of a fraction of their own vitality.  
- Military doctrine relies on iron broadhead arrows and pike formations to neutralize hostile casters, a tactic born of repeated conflicts with the Empire.

### ⚙️ The Aethelian Empire – *Order Through Will*
**Government:** Autocracy under the Iron Sovereign, advised by the Magisterium of state-sanctioned mages.  
**Capital:** Ferrax, a metropolis of black iron and basalt, ringed by legion encampments.  
**Culture:** Militaristic, hierarchical, meritocratic in its bureaucracy. Magic is a science, a tool of imperial expansion and control.  
**Magical Practice:**  
- All three disciplines are taught as branches of arcane engineering. **Evocation** is artillery, **Abjuration** is fortification, **Alchemy & Infusion** is materiel.  
- The Empire’s innovation is the **legion-battery system**: battle-mages are accompanied by supply cohorts carrying pre-charged gemstone Mediums. The caster pays almost no personal Toll; the empire pays in resources and logistics.  
- The Magisterium mass-produces alchemical weapons — fire-resistant mantles, unbreakable siege ladders, flesh-searing swords.  
- The Iron Lictors, the emperor’s personal guard, wear full suits of cold iron armor, making them utterly immune to magic and the ultimate enforcers of imperial will. Imperial prisons are iron-plated fortresses where no mage can cast.

### 🏜️ Dominion of Khaz-Mora – *The Flame Endures*
**Government:** Magocratic oligarchy. The Conclave of Archons, sorcerer-lords bound to elemental pacts, rules absolutely. Society is divided into castes based on magical sensitivity.  
**Capital:** Irem-Kaz, a city of obsidian ziggurats and ever-burning braziers, hidden deep in the desert.  
**Culture:** Fatalistic, esoteric, endurance-obsessed. The Archons are effectively immortal — but at a terrible cost.  
**Magical Practice:**  
- The Dominion’s signature is a perverse mastery of **Alchemy & Infusion**. The Archons sustain their immortality through a continuous, hidden working that siphons life force from the land and the unsensitive castes, paying the Toll with the vitality of an entire nation.  
- Their famous Arcanum crystals are not mined but *grown* from distilled life essence. Refining them is a soul-scouring process, making each crystal a tiny, screaming battery of stolen life.  
- **Evocation** is used sparingly, often via golems and infused constructs rather than direct casting. **Abjuration** seals their tombs and temples.  
- Whispers-assassins are trained to attack the **Mind’s Eye** — using neurotoxins, terror-inflicting powders, and concussion traps to make their targets unable to focus, turning a mage’s own power against them.

## The Accordance (Neutral Ground)
The central basin, demilitarized and governed by the Treaty of Five Thrones. No standing army may enter. Fortifications are forbidden. Peace is enforced by the Sentinels.

### Midden – The Crossroad City
A sprawling, polyglot trade hub of 100,000 souls at the confluence of the two great rivers.  
- **Districts:** The Embassies (fortified townhouses of each power), the Shaded Market (anything is for sale), the Archive (law, records, translation), the Warrens (refugees, exiles, heretics).  
- **Rule of Midden:** Open warfare is forbidden. Espionage, however, is an accepted sport. The Concord Council chambers are lined entirely with cold iron, creating a dead-magic zone where ambassadors must negotiate as equals, without spell or cantrip.

### The Sentinel Order
A secretive brotherhood of blind monks who have guarded the Accordance since before the Treaty.  
- Their blindness is a willing sacrifice; initiation involves an **Infusion** that links their life force to the God-Fall mechanism, enhancing their **Mind’s Eye** to perceive magical tampering across the continent.  
- Their fortress-monastery, the **Eyrie**, floats above Midden on a disc of petrified cloud, invisible to scrying.  
- They command silent, golem-like **Arbiters** — alchemical constructs of immense power that absorb any magic cast illegally in the Accordance, paying the Toll themselves and storing the energy.  
- The Order is neutral, answerable only to its own ancient charter. The Speaker of the Concord Council is always a Sentinel.

### Academy of the Balanced Flame
The only institution in Vaedris where the Three Disciplines are taught together, without state or religious bias. Founded by the Treaty to prevent any one power from monopolizing magical knowledge.  
- **Location:** A sprawling campus on the western bank of the Silvervein River, within Midden’s outer walls but diplomatically extraterritorial.  
- **Students & Faculty:** Accepted from all five powers, provided they forswear national allegiance during their tenure. The faculty is a mixture of former Magisterium defectors, Frostmark rune-sages, Valeris abjurers, and a handful of Dominion exiles.  
- **Curriculum:** Emphasizes the **Law of Conservation** as the central tenet. Graduates are trained to be mindful of the Toll and to respect the Great Limitations. Military applications of magic are taught only theoretically.  
- **The Iron Halls:** Practice rooms are lined with cold iron, forcing students to learn to cast without relying on external Mediums — a grueling test of personal stamina and focus.

### Archmage’s Tower
A soaring spire of white stone and silver filigree at the exact center of Midden’s Concord Square. It serves as the residence and laboratory of the **Archmage of the Accordance**.  
- The Archmage is appointed by the Concord Council (with a Sentinel veto) and must be a master of all three disciplines, a scholar of unblemished neutrality.  
- The Tower houses the **Grand Orrery**, a model of the heavens and leylines used to forecast magical weather and detect continent-scale imbalances.  
- The Archmage advises the Council on all arcane matters, mediates magical disputes between the powers, and holds the **Key of Nullification** — an artifact capable of temporarily extending the dead-magic field of the Council Chamber across the entire city in an emergency.  
- By tradition, the Archmage never leaves the Accordance; the Tower’s iron-plated sublevels serve as their tomb.

### The Neutral Ground’s Role
The Accordance does not appear in the table because it is not a sovereign power, but every relationship is mediated through it. Midden’s Embassies are where the real diplomacy happens, the Academy produces graduates who return home with cross-cultural training (or are promptly recruited as spies), and the Archmage’s Tower serves as the emergency circuit-breaker when magical tensions threaten the continent.
