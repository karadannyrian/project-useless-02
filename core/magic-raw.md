## The Core Laws of Magic

### 1. The Law of Conservation (The Toll)

Magic cannot be created out of nothing, nor can it disappear without a trace. Every spell requires a source of energy, and every action has an equal and opposite reaction.

* **The Price:** Channeling magic drains the caster’s physical stamina, body heat, or vital energy. Fire magic might leave a wizard shivering with hypothermia; kinetic magic might fracture the caster's own bones if the force is too great.
* **The Medium:** To circumvent personal toll, mages use external batteries—like refined gemstones, rare metals, or ambient environmental heat.

### 2. The Law of Sympathy (Like Produces Like)

The classic rule of connection. An effect can be produced by mimicking it, or by manipulating a piece of the target.

* **Similitude:** A miniature clay model of a gate can be used to bar or break the real gate miles away.
* **Contagion:** Items once in physical contact retain a spiritual connection. A strand of a target’s hair or a drop of their blood makes a spell infinitely more potent and impossible to dodge.

### 3. The Law of True Names (The Nominal Bound)

To control a thing completely, you must know its cosmic, primordial name. Everything in existence—every rock, bird, person, and gust of wind—has a hidden name that describes its absolute essence.

* **The Trap:** Speaking a True Name gives the caster absolute dominion over that object or person.
* **The Defense:** True Names change as a person grows, learns, or suffers, making them incredibly difficult to discover or maintain power over.

## The Three Disciplines of the Arcane

Traditional magic is generally divided into three strict schools of practice, depending on how the mage interacts with the world:

| Discipline | Mechanism | Typical Use Case |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Evocation** | Converting internal or external energy into raw physical elements. | Conjuring localized storms, shaping stone, creating light. |
| **Abjuration** | Rewriting the structural integrity of space to deny entry or force. | Warding a room against sound, creating physical shields, breaking curses. |
| **Alchemy & Infusion** | Binding magical energy permanently into physical matter. | Forging blades that never dull, creating drafts that accelerate healing. |

## The Great Limitations & Vulnerabilities

Even the most powerful Archmage is bound by strict universal constants. If a wizard breaks these rules, the universe corrects itself violently.

* **The Threshold of Life:** Magic cannot truly resurrect the dead. It can reanimate a corpse into a mindless thrall, but the soul, once departed, is beyond the reach of mortal arcana.
* **The Iron Allergy:** Cold iron disrupts the flow of magical energy. It acts as a grounding wire, grounding out spells. A mage bound in iron chains is entirely powerless.
* **The Mind's Eye:** Magic requires absolute focus and mental visualization. If a mage is concussed, drugged, or gripped by sheer panic, their spells will fizzle or, worse, backfire into chaotic **wild magic**.
