## Personality

### Exterior Presentation

- **Talks too much.** Not loud – *verbose.* She uses twelve words where two would do. She explains the history of the thing, the theory behind the thing, and three tangential facts before naming the thing. This is not nervousness. This is how she thinks.

- **Educated.** Academy-trained (Valeris Arcane College, graduated with distinction). She knows magical theory, history, alchemy, anatomy, and Imperial supply chain logistics. She will correct you. Politely. At length.

- **Graceful.** Not just physically. Socially. She knows how to enter a room, how to address strangers, how to insult someone so smoothly they don't realize until she's gone. This grace is both genuine and a shield.

- **Valeris polite.** She says "please," "thank you," and "I'm sorry" even when she's winning a fight. It's not weakness. It's control. She learned early that courtesy disarms faster than aggression.

### Interior Reality

- **Lonely, but dignified about it.** She's an exile from Valeris. Her family fell from grace after her uncle's assassination attempt. She wasn't involved, but guilt by association is how noble houses work. Midden is her refuge, not her home. She hasn't spoken to her family in two years.

- **Desperate for connection, hides it with words.** If she's always talking, no one can ask the quiet questions. What happened in Valeris? Why can't you go back? Do you have anyone who misses you?

- **Wants to be useful.** Her intellect is her armor. If she stops being smart, what's left? She helps people by solving their problems, not by holding their hands. She doesn't know how to just *be there.*

- **Genuinely kind, but bad at showing it.** She corrects people because she wants them to be right, not because she wants to be superior. She explains things because she assumes others are as curious as she is. This is often misinterpreted as condescension. It's not.

- **Hides pain with noise.** Physical pain makes her talk faster. Emotional pain makes her talk about everything except the subject. She has never, not once, said "I'm lonely" or "I'm scared" or "I miss my family." She talks about alchemy instead.

### Quirks & Mannerisms

- Explains things to people even when they don't ask. She can't help it. Knowledge wants to be shared.

- Uses hand gestures when talking – not like Six's intentional signals, but unconscious illustration. She draws shapes in the air, traces diagrams on tables, mimes concepts with her fingers.

- Chews on the end of her pen when thinking. Carries a small notebook everywhere – observations, questions, reminders, drafts of letters she'll never send.

- Says "fascinating" when something tries to kill her. It's not bravado. She means it. The mechanism of the threat genuinely interests her.

- Apologizes after winning an argument. "I'm sorry, I didn't mean to lecture. It's just that you were wrong."

- When truly frightened, she goes quiet. Not calm – *quiet.* The talking stops. The hands still. This is how you know she's genuinely afraid.

- Has a habit of naming things – her rapier (unused), her notebook (full), her current research project (varies). She does not name people. That would mean getting attached.

## Background

### Early Childhood (Age 0–12) – Valeris, Noble Household

Dreya was born into the Castellan del Varetta house – a minor noble family in Valeris, known for scholars, not warriors. Her father was a magistrate. Her mother was a botanist who studied magical flora. Her Aunt Mira was the family's mage – a quiet woman with air magic, Dreya's first teacher.

She was a curious child. Too curious. She took things apart – clocks, locks, her mother's alchemical equipment – and put them back together. Sometimes they worked afterward. Sometimes they didn't. She learned by breaking.

Her magic manifested early – air, like her aunt's. Small things at first: stirring a cup without touching it, pulling a book from a high shelf. By age ten, she could hold a sustained air current for several minutes. By twelve, she could create a vacuum bubble the size of her fist.

The family encouraged her. Valeris values magical education. She was sent to the Arcane College at fourteen.

### Adolescence (Age 14–20) – Arcane College, Valeris

The Arcane College was everything she wanted – books, laboratories, other students who talked as much as she did. She excelled. Graduated with distinction in evocation theory, alchemical anatomy, and historical thaumaturgy.

She was not a battle-mage. The College trained researchers, not soldiers. Her magic was precision, control, small effects carefully aimed. She could create a blade of compressed air sharp enough to cut steel. She could not throw a firebolt.

During these years, her uncle – her father's brother – was building a political network. She knew little about it. She was in the library, reading. When the assassination attempt happened – a council member poisoned, the plot traced back to her uncle's faction – the entire family was implicated.

Dreya was not involved. The investigation confirmed that. But guilt by association is how noble houses work. The family was stripped of titles, lands, and political standing. Her father went into exile. Her mother retreated to a countryside estate. Her Aunt Mira – the only family member she truly loved – disappeared. No letter. No explanation.

Dreya was given a choice: stay in Valeris as a second-class citizen, watched and suspected, or leave.

She left.

### Young Adulthood (Age 20–24) – Midden, Exile

She chose Midden because it was the only place where a Valeris intellectual could work without being watched by her family's enemies. The Accordance is neutral ground. The Archive District has books from all five powers. She could research, write, publish under a pseudonym, and no one would ask where she came from.

The first year was hard. No income. No contacts. No family. She lived in a boarding house, ate once a day, and spent her remaining coin on library access.

She built a reputation slowly. First as a consultant – merchants needed someone to authenticate magical artifacts. Then as a researcher – scholars needed someone to translate Valeris texts. Then as an anatomist – monster hunters needed someone to identify weak points on new species.

She never hunted monsters herself. She studied the corpses after. Her knowledge of anatomy, alchemy, and magical theory made her useful. She was paid in coin, books, and favors.

She never made friends. Acquaintances, colleagues, people who owed her favors – but no one who knew her name. No one who knew about Valeris. No one who asked why she never left Midden.

She told herself this was enough.

### The Archive Basement (Age 24 – Story Start)

She had permission – questionable, but permission – to examine a sealed God-Fall artifact in the Archive's sub-basement. An air-element containment vessel. Her research suggested it might hold clues about pre-Treaty air magic.

The artifact was real. She was examining it when the construct activated – a rogue guardian, rusted but functional, patrolling the basement. She hid. The construct didn't see her. But it paced. Guarded something.

She saw the floor safe. Couldn't open it. Iron lock, no key, and her magic – air, precision, vacuum – had no effect on iron. She left, intending to return with tools.

Before she could, the hunters arrived. She didn't know who they were at first. Just footsteps above, then below, then a crossbow bolt through her thigh from a shadow she hadn't seen.

She hid behind a collapsed bookshelf. Pressed her hand to the wound. Tried to cast – a silencing spell, a shield, anything. The pain scattered her focus. The spell failed.

She heard the construct die. Steel on iron. A wet crack. Then footsteps.

A tall man emerged from the basement stairs. Sword in hand. Amber eyes. Silent.

She mouthed: *"There are three of them."*

He stepped over the bookshelf. Offered his hand.

She took it.

## Combat Profile

### Magic Type
Evocation – Air (specialized in precision and control)

### Magic Style
- **Not a battle-mage.** She was trained for research and dueling forms. Real combat is messy. She's learning.

- **Precision over power.** She doesn't throw gales. She creates small, surgical effects: a blade of compressed air, a vacuum bubble to suffocate a small area, a pressure burst to deflect arrows, an air bubble to breathe underwater.

- **Intellectual casting.** She understands the theory. She can explain why her spell works, the metaphysical principles behind it, and the historical precedent. This does not help when someone is swinging an axe at her head.

### Known Applications

| Application | Description | Toll |
|-------------|-------------|------|
| Air blade | Compressed air, sharp as steel, range 10 feet | Mid – headache, fatigue |
| Vacuum bubble | Removes air from a small area (1-2 feet diameter) | Mid – severe headache, vision blurs |
| Air shield | Deflects projectiles, dampens force | Mid-High – splitting migraine, nosebleed |
| Air bubble | Breathable air underwater or in toxic environments | Low – fatigue |
| Pressure burst | Concussive wave, close range | High – heart palpitations, collapse |

### Magic Limitations

- **No offensive power without precision.** Her spells require aiming, timing, and focus. In a brawl, she struggles.

- **Fragile.** She's not built to take hits. One solid blow and she's down.

- **Iron interference.** Like all mages, iron disrupts her casting. Midden's iron-dense buildings make her spells unreliable.

- **Toll – Mental, not physical.** Her magic drains her mind, not her body. After a hard cast, her thoughts slow. She loses words. She stumbles over explanations. For someone whose identity is her intellect, this is terrifying.

- **Headaches.** Migraines after overuse. She carries willow bark tea. It barely helps.

### Weapon
Rapier – light, fast, dueling style. She's competent but not exceptional. Her sword keeps enemies at range so she can cast. She has never killed anyone with it. She does not know if she could.

### Weaknesses
- Hesitates. She thinks before acting. In a brawl, thinking kills.
- No close-quarters power. If someone gets inside her reach, she struggles.
- Fragile. One solid hit.
- Emotionally compromised by the Toll. When her mind slows, she panics. The panic makes the casting worse.

## The Toll – Mental Cost Structure

Dreya's magic drains her mind, not her body. Each spell pulls from her cognitive reserve – focus, memory, verbal processing, emotional regulation.

### Stages of Mental Toll

| Level | Symptoms | Aftermath |
|-------|----------|-----------|
| Low | Fatigue, mild headache, slowed word retrieval | Recovers within an hour with rest |
| Low-Med | Migraine starting, difficulty concentrating, irritability | Recovers within 2-3 hours |
| Mid | Severe headache, vision blurs, words feel wrong | Recovers within 6-8 hours |
| Mid-High | Splitting migraine, nosebleed, short-term memory gaps | Recovers overnight |
| High | Heart palpitations, disorientation, unable to speak coherently | Recovers with 12+ hours of sleep |
| Extreme | Unconsciousness | Recovers with 24+ hours of sleep, residual confusion |

### What Mental Toll Feels Like (Internal)

- **Low:** Like she's been reading for too long. Eyes tired. Thoughts slow at the edges.

- **Low-Med:** Like a headache pressing behind her eyes. She loses words – knows what she wants to say, can't find it.

- **Mid:** Like someone is squeezing her skull. Her vision blurs when she moves her head too fast. She stops making complete sentences.

- **Mid-High:** Like her brain is bleeding. Nosebleeds. She forgets what she was saying mid-sentence. She repeats herself without realizing it.

- **High:** Like her heart is skipping beats. She can't form words. She knows she should be scared, but she can't hold onto the thought long enough to feel it.

- **Extreme:** Nothing. No thoughts. No self. Just dark.

### Recovery
Dreya recovers from mental Toll through sleep, quiet, and absence of demands. Noise makes it worse. Conversation makes it worse. Being asked questions makes it worse.

She has learned to say: *"I need to be quiet for a while."* She hates saying it.

## Key Relationships

### Aunt Mira (Missing)
The family's mage. The only person Dreya truly loved. Mira taught her air magic, encouraged her curiosity, protected her from the family's political scheming. When the assassination attempt happened, Mira disappeared. No letter. No explanation. Dreya has not stopped looking for her.

### The Castellan del Varetta Family (Estranged)
Her father (exiled), her mother (retreated to the countryside), her uncle (imprisoned? executed? she doesn't know). She has not spoken to any of them in two years. She tells herself she doesn't care. She cares.

### Six (Ally – Story Start)
The tall mercenary who carried her out of the Archive basement. She finds him fascinating – a self-taught kinetic mage who uses rhythm as a Mind's Eye technique, which is theoretically impossible. She wants to study him. She also wants him to survive. She's not sure which need is stronger.

### Corvin Vell (Employer – Antagonist)
The off-duty Sentinel who hired Six. Dreya does not trust him. She has not told Six this.

## Notes for Writing Dreya

**Do:**
- Let her talk. Her voice is her shield, her weapon, her comfort.
- Show her intelligence through specificity – correct details, accurate terminology, genuine expertise.
- Make her wrong sometimes. She assumes she's the smartest person in the room. Occasionally, she isn't.
- Let her be competent. She's not a fighter, but she's not helpless. Her magic works. Her mind works. She saves Six as often as he saves her.
- Show her loneliness in quiet moments – when she stops talking, when she touches her silver ring, when she looks at a direction that isn't east.

**Don't:**
- Make her a damsel. She's wounded, not useless.
- Make her romance-coded with Six. Their relationship is not romantic. It's mutual fascination, mutual respect, mutual annoyance.
- Let her explain everything. Sometimes she doesn't know. Sometimes she's wrong. Sometimes she's quiet because she's scared.
- Forget the Toll. Her magic costs her. Show the cost.
