# The *Grey-Eye* and Her Crew

---

## The Ship

The *Grey-Eye* is a twin-masted whaling schooner out of Midden, sixty feet at the waterline, built from ironwood and spite. She's old—older than her captain, older than Midden's last three dockmasters—and she shows it in the way her timbers groan in a heavy sea, in the hogged curve of her keel, in the patches on her sails that outnumber the original cloth. She's not fast. She's not pretty. She's *seaworthy*, which is a different thing entirely.

Her hull is stained grey from decades of salt and whale oil, and her figurehead—a woman with one eye open and one eye closed—has been battered by ice until her features are more suggestion than sculpture. Sailors in the Midden docks call her the *Grey-Eye* because she always seems to know where the whales are before any other ship. The captain knows why. The captain isn't telling.

She smells of rendered blubber, tar, and the particular funk of men who share a forecastle for months at a time. Reks thinks of her as *she* and not *it*. She's the only home he has.

---

## The Captain: Orris Vane

**Age:** 54
**Role:** Master of the *Grey-Eye*, keeper of secrets

Orris Vane is a hatchet-faced woman with grey hair cropped short as a deckhand's and eyes the color of weathered copper. She lost two fingers on her left hand to a harpoon line thirty years ago and has never bothered to hide the stumps. She's run the *Grey-Eye* for two decades, and in that time she's lost fewer crew than any whaler in the Midden fleet. She knows why. She hired Reks when he was nineteen, half-starved and lying about his age, because she recognized the Corway look and understood what it meant.

She's never mentioned this to him. She pays him fairly, works him hard, and asks no questions about the things he sees in the rigging. When the Dominion started asking around about a blonde, blue-eyed whaler with an uncanny survival record, she told them she didn't know anyone matching that description. She lied smoothly, without hesitation, and went back to her charts.

Her only visible tell: she chews wintermint leaves constantly, even when they're out of season, even when she has to pay extortionate prices for them in Midden. The habit has stained her teeth faintly green.

**Voice:** Terse, profane, unexpectedly warm with her crew. She calls Reks "boy" despite him being twenty-three. She's the only person alive he allows to do this.

**Function in the story:** Safe harbor. The one adult who's never betrayed him. She'll lie to the Dominion for him, hide him when he needs hiding, and tell him when he's being an idiot.

## The Bosun: Tamas Kelm

**Age:** 61
**Role:** Bosun, rigging master, reluctant father figure

Tamas Kelm is a Frostmark ex-pat who left the north forty years ago and has never said why. He's broad-shouldered and heavy-bellied, with a white beard he braids with tarred twine and arms that are still strong enough to haul a grown man out of the water one-handed. His left ear is mostly gone—frostbite, he claims, though the scar looks more like a knife wound—and his right knee predicts storms with alarming accuracy.

He taught Reks to splice rope, to read weather, to navigate by stars when the compass failed. He never asked where the boy came from or why his eyes went strange during the hunts. He just handed him a marline spike and said, "Do it again. Tighter."

Tamas knows what Reks is. He knew the Corway name before it was proscribed, before the purge, before the village burned. He's never said this aloud. But sometimes, when Reks is aloft in the rigging, Tamas watches him with an expression that's too heavy for a bosun assessing a deckhand's competence. It's the look of a man who made a promise to someone, decades ago, and is still keeping it.

**Voice:** Gravelly, patient, prone to saying things like *"The sea doesn't care about your feelings"* and meaning it as comfort.

**Function in the story:** The anchor. The one who knows the old stories but won't tell them until Reks asks. The one who might have known Reks' grandmother.

## The Harpooneer: Drenn Olsk

**Age:** 26
**Role:** Primary harpooneer, rival, walking complication

Drenn is what Reks would be if Reks had been born to a living family with a legitimate name. He's Frostmark stock—broad, fair-haired, blue-eyed in the ordinary way, not the Corway way—and he's been on the *Grey-Eye* for three years. He's good at his job. He's loud about being good at his job. He doesn't like Reks.

The dislike is professional on the surface. Drenn thinks Reks is reckless in the chase-boats. He thinks the captain gives him special treatment. He thinks it's suspicious that a rigger with no family and no history can predict whale-paths with such accuracy. He's not wrong about any of this.

Underneath, the dislike is simpler: Drenn is afraid. He's seen Reks' eyes go strange during a hunt. He's seen him move before a whale sounded, before anyone else could have known. He's heard the old sailors' songs about the Corway heresy, and he's put pieces together that he doesn't fully understand. Fear makes him cruel. Cruelty makes him dangerous.

**Voice:** Blustering, confrontational, occasionally insightful despite himself. He's not stupid. He's just angry about things he can't name.

**Function in the story:** Friction. The crew member most likely to sell Reks out if the price is right—and the crew member most likely to regret it afterward. A reminder that the Corway legacy isn't just a danger from outside forces.

## The Cabin Boy: Pip

**Age:** 14
**Role:** Cabin boy, cook's assistant, unacknowledged ward

Pip doesn't have a surname, or if he does, he doesn't remember it. He was a dock-rat in Midden, a pickpocket and runner for one of the Shaded Market fences, until he tried to lift Reks' coin purse three years ago. Reks caught him by the wrist, looked at him for a long moment—this scrawny, dirt-smeared child with hunger-sharp cheekbones and eyes that were already too old—and instead of breaking his fingers, bought him a meal.

Pip has been on the *Grey-Eye* ever since. He's the cabin boy, which means he does everything no one else wants to do: scrubbing pots, emptying slop buckets, running messages, learning knots. He's small for his age, quick and quiet, and he hero-worships Reks with an intensity that embarrasses them both.

Reks has never admitted that he sees himself in Pip. The orphan, the survivor, the child who learned too early that the world doesn't care. He's trying to give Pip a better version of what he got—a crew, a trade, a place to belong. He doesn't know if it's working.

**Voice:** Eager, irreverent, prone to asking questions no one else will. Calls Reks "Reks" to his face and "the harpoon bastard" behind his back with roughly equal affection.

**Function in the story:** The stakes. The person Reks is trying to protect. The proof that Reks is capable of care even when his FID won't let him name it. If Pip is threatened, Reks will burn down everything.

---

## The Ship's Cat: Mote (Current)

**Role:** Rat control, morale, emotional support

There has always been a ship's cat on the *Grey-Eye*, and the cat is always called Mote. Reks named the current one—a scarred grey tom with one torn ear and a purr like a bilge pump—after the cat from his village. He told himself it was practical. Ship needs a cat. Cat needs a name. Mote was available.

He doesn't examine why his chest tightens when the cat chooses to sleep in his hammock instead of anyone else's.

**Function in the story:** A small, quiet thread connecting Reks to the past he won't name. Proof that some things survive.

| Crew Member | Role | Relationship to Reks | Risk Level |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Orris Vane** | Captain | Protector, secret-keeper | Low—actively shields him |
| **Tamas Kelm** | Bosun | Teacher, possible link to the old Corway world | Low—but knows more than he says |
| **Drenn Olsk** | Harpooneer | Rival, suspicious | High—fear makes him volatile |
| **Pip** | Cabin boy | Ward, unspoken surrogate brother | Critical—Reks' moral anchor |
